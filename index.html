About the Solar System

The Solar System consists of the Sun and the celestial objects bound to it by gravity, including the eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets like Pluto, comets, and asteroids.

The planets in order from the Sun are: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

It formed about 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud.
